SUGARCANE

• The best rainfall pattern for sugarcane is a moderate and consistent distribution throughout its growth period. Extreme weather conditions, such as heavy rainfall or prolonged drought, can adversely affect sugarcane yields.
• Sugarcane can be grown in various types of soil, but the most suitable soil is one that is well-drained, loamy, and rich in nutrients.
• Sugarcane needs warm temperatures to grow properly, but it can tolerate some cooler and hotter periods. Sugarcane grows best in temperatures between 75°F and 95°F (24°C to 35°C) during the growing season.
• Drip irrigation with subsurface drip lines involves burying drip lines beneath the soil surface along the sugarcane rows. This maximizes water efficiency and minimizes evaporation.
