COTTON

The best rainfall pattern is moderate and consistent throughout the growth period. If there's too much rain, it can damage the crop. If there's too little rain, it can cause the cotton to dry out and not grow properly.
• Cotton can be grown in various types of soil, but the most suitable soil is one that is well-drained, loamy, and rich in nutrients.
Cotton grows in the warm season, typically during the summer months when temperatures are moderate to warm. Extreme temperatures, both hot and cold, can harm the cotton crop.
• Furrow irrigation involves creating small channels (furrows) between cotton rows and allowing water to flow through them, directly to the plant roots. This ensures efficient use of water and minimizes soil erosion.
