WHEAT

• Wheat has different water needs. The amount of water it needs depends on several factors such as how often it rains, the type of wheat, the kind of soil it's planted in, the temperature, and the amount of water available in the irrigation system and the soil. If there's not enough water, the wheat might not yield as much grain.
• Extreme temperatures, both hot and cold, can harm the wheat crop. Wheat grows in the cool season, during fall, winter, and early spring when temperatures are moderate. In areas with extreme temperatures, it may be necessary to adjust the planting time to avoid the hottest or coldest periods.
• Wheat needs around 15 to 25 inches (380 to 635 mm) of rainfall during its growing season. Rainfall should be well-distributed, especially during germination, tillering, and flowering stages. The best rainfall pattern is moderate and consistent throughout the growth period.
• Selecting the right soil is crucial for growing wheat. Wheat can be grown in various types of soil, but the most suitable soil is one that is loamy soil
• Pulse irrigation is used where water is supplied in repeated burst in short interval. This maintains soil moisture and prevents water logging
